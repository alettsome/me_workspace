### 1. Key Health Claims

- **Weight Loss is Simple and Safe:** The plan asserts that weight control involves only two factors: knowledge of simple nutrition rules and willpower. It claims users will lose 1-3 pounds per week safely by eating fewer calories than the body burns, forcing it to use stored fat.
- **No Drugs, No Hunger:** The title and text emphasize that the diet requires no drugs and does not cause hunger, largely due to the appetite-curbing properties of the Knox Gelatine drink.
- **Health Improvement, Not Just Weight Loss:** The diet is claimed to "actually improve your health as well as control your weight," based on sound nutritional principles used for diabetic patients.
- **Protein is a Vital Key:** The book heavily promotes protein, claiming the body cannot store it and needs a daily supply to "build and renew body tissues." Without enough protein during reducing, "body tissues waste away, muscles sag and pep lags."
- **The Knox Drink is Essential:** The Knox High-Protein Gelatine Drink is claimed to work in three ways: as a refreshing, filling snack; to curb appetite; and to supply "additional protein, a must when you are taking off excess weight."
- **Long-Term Slimness:** The plan claims that following the diet will form "sensible eating habits" that help users lose their "desire for over-eating" and maintain their new weight.

### 2. Dietary Recommendations

- **The "Choice-of-Foods" System:** This is the core of the plan. Foods are divided into six exchange lists (Vegetable, Bread, Milk, Fat, Fruit, Meat). Foods within each list are "nutritional equals" with the same amounts of protein, carbohydrate, fat, and calories. Users select a specific number of "choices" from each list per day based on their calorie target (1200, 1600, or 1800).
- **High-Protein Emphasis:** The diet recommends a high protein intake, specifically through the Meat List (beef, poultry, fish, eggs, cheese) and the Knox Gelatine drink.
- **The Knox High-Protein Drink:** This is a central recommendation, to be taken three times a day, 30 minutes before meals. It can be made as a cold drink (with fruit juice or water) or a hot drink (with bouillon). After reaching the weight goal, a "Booster" version made with non-fat dry milk is recommended.
- **"Between-Meal" Snacks:** Snacks are not only allowed but described as "necessary" for an "energy lift" and to "dull the appetite." The Knox drink is the primary recommended snack.
- **Low-Calorie Substitutes:** The plan recommends using non-caloric sweeteners (saccharin, Sucaryl, Sweeta), skim milk, unsweetened canned fruits, and low-calorie salad dressings.
- **Specific Foods Allowed:** The plan includes "favorites such as steak, baked potatoes, seafood and delicious desserts" (the desserts being Knox gelatine-based recipes).
- **Portion Control and Preparation:** The plan emphasizes careful measuring with standard cups and spoons, trimming fat from meat, and avoiding added fats or flour in cooking.

### 3. Evidence Cited

- **Medical Authority:** The plan's primary evidence is an appeal to medical authority. It states the diet is based on "sound nutritional principles with which your doctor has had a great deal of experience" and that the Food Exchange Lists have been used for years by the medical profession for diabetic patients.
- **Scientific Basis:** It claims the Food Exchanges were "carefully and scientifically developed" and that "science proves" the importance of protein.
- **Caloric Deficit:** The basic principle of weight loss is explained as a simple energy balance: "you’ve been eating more food than your body burns up... so, this extra food has turned to fat."
- **Professional Endorsement:** The footnotes cite the "American Diabetes Association, Inc., and The American Dietetic Association" as the source for the Food Exchange Lists, and the Metropolitan Life Insurance Company for the desirable weight charts.
- **Anecdotal/Implied Success:** The text implies success by stating the diet will "solve your weight problem" and that users will see a "dramatic change in your looks and your feelings."

### 4. Core Themes

- **Simplicity and Ease of Use:** A major theme is that the diet is not complicated. The "Choice-of-Foods" system is presented as a way to avoid calorie counting and worrying about balanced meals, as the "work has been done for you."
- **Normalcy and Variety:** The plan strongly contrasts itself with "monotonous 'same foods every day' diets" or "extreme or faddish ones." It promises a wide variety of normal, family-friendly foods.
- **Medical Legitimacy:** The entire plan is framed as being doctor-approved and based on established medical and nutritional science, specifically linking it to diabetic meal planning to convey authority and safety.
- **Self-Improvement and Aesthetics:** Weight loss is tied to improved appearance ("slender, new figure," "more attractive"), feeling "younger, healthier, happier," and increased energy ("pep").
