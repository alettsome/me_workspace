Based on the provided text of "A Guide to Health" by Mahatma Gandhi, here is a structured summary of its health and nutrition claims.

### 1. Key Health Claims

- **Health is Holistic:** True health is "a sound mind in a sound body." A pure character and controlled mind are the foundation of health; evil thoughts and passions are forms of disease.
- **Disease is Internal Filth:** Illness is Nature's warning that filth has accumulated in the body. The goal is to help Nature remove this filth, not to suppress symptoms with medicine.
- **Medicines are Harmful:** Medicines are largely useless and often harmful. They cover up the problem, making Nature's task harder. More people die from medicines than from diseases themselves.
- **Impure Air is the Root Cause:** Impure air is the cause of disease in 99 out of 100 cases. Most people breathe far more impure air than they drink dirty water or eat dirty food.
- **Overeating is a Major Sin:** 99% of people eat more than they need. Overeating is a primary cause of illness, leading to a weak, diseased body and a dull mind. It is also a moral failing, as it steals food from the poor.
- **Pandering to the Palate is Slavery:** Eating for pleasure rather than sustenance is a root cause of other sins (lying, stealing, etc.) and is a sign of being lower than animals.
- **Incontinence (Lack of Brahmacharya) is the Greatest Threat:** The loss of vital fluid through sexual indulgence is the primary cause of physical and mental weakness. Conserving this energy is the "one thing needful" for true health and strength.

### 2. Dietary Recommendations

- **Ideal Diet:** A fruit-based diet (fruits, nuts, and oil) is the best and most natural for humans, as it aligns with our anatomy (like fruit-eating monkeys) and provides all necessary nutrients.
- **Recommended Foods:**
    - **Fruits:** Plantain, date, grape, apple, almond, walnut, groundnut, cocoanut, orange, lime.
    - **Grains:** Wheat is the best cereal. It should be freshly ground at home and used without sifting. Rice is considered "quite useless as a food."
    - **Oils:** Olive oil is recommended as a healthy fat and a substitute for ghee.
    - **Drink:** A substitute for tea/coffee can be made from fried, powdered wheat.
- **Foods to be Eschewed (Avoided Completely):**
    - **Stimulants & Intoxicants:** Wine, bhang, opium, tobacco, tea, coffee, and cocoa. All are considered poisonous and injurious to health and character.
    - **Spices & Condiments (Masala):** Pepper, mustard, coriander, etc. They create artificial hunger and lead to indigestion and disease.
    - **Salt:** Considered unnecessary and harmful. Nature provides enough salt in vegetables. Disuse of salt is said to benefit diseases like piles and asthma.
    - **Milk:** Should be avoided after infancy. It is difficult to keep pure, can transmit disease from the cow, and is unnatural for adults. Almond milk is a recommended substitute.
    - **Meat:** Not the natural food of man. It generates harmful acids, leads to physical decay (teeth, rheumatism), and stirs up evil passions.
    - **Pulses (Dhall):** Heavy and hard to digest. They generate acid in the system, leading to disease and premature old age.
- **Eating Patterns:**
    - Eat only to satisfy hunger, not the palate.
    - **Masticate food thoroughly** until it is mixed with saliva.
    - Eat only **two meals a day**; skip breakfast.
    - **Fast at least once a fortnight** to give the digestive system a rest.
    - Avoid all feasts and the social pressure to overeat.

### 3. Evidence Cited

- **Personal Experience (30 years):** Gandhi repeatedly states his conclusions are based on his own long-term experiments, including his fruit-only diet, giving up salt, and his observations of his own past overeating.
- **Anecdotal Evidence:** Stories of women becoming ill without tea, a man dying from overeating pepper, a doctor tricking a patient into walking, and Count Tolstoy's story of a man committing murder after smoking.
- **Observations of Nature:** The argument that animals eat only to appease hunger, that a calf stops drinking milk after getting teeth, and that the farmer's life is the "natural life" for man.
- **Analogy:** The body is like a house; medicines cover up filth like a dirty house. The stomach is like a steam engine; impure air is like bad coal.
- **Cited Authorities (without formal citations):**
    - **Doctors:** Sir Astley Cooper, Dr. Baker, Dr. Frank, Dr. Masongood (all critical of medicine). Dr. Haig (on the harms of pulses and meat). Dr. Kingsford (on meat). Dr. Deway (on fasting).
    - **Writers:** Milton, Thoreau (on walking), Justice Stephen (on ignorance of drugs).
    - **Scientists:** Unnamed scientists who claim fruits have all necessary elements and that cooking destroys nutrients.
- **Structural Argument:** The human body's anatomy (teeth, stomach) is similar to fruit-eating animals, implying a natural vegetable/fruit diet.

### 4. Core Themes

- **Nature as the Ultimate Guide:** Health is achieved by living in accordance with natural laws. Nature provides pure air, water, and the right food. Civilization and modern habits (closed rooms, processed food, medicines) are the primary causes of disease.
- **Mind-Body Connection:** Physical health is inseparable from mental and moral purity. Controlling the senses (especially the palate and sexual urge) is the path to true, lasting health.
- **Simplicity and Self-Reliance:** The healthiest life is the simplest. One should grow their own food (or eat simple, local foods), walk instead of ride, wear minimal, loose clothing, and avoid doctors and medicines. The individual is responsible for their own health.
- **Anti-Materialism and Anti-Consumerism:** The pursuit of pleasure (rich food, fashionable dress, jewelry, tobacco, tea) is condemned as a source of disease, moral decay, and economic waste. This includes a critique of exploitative labor practices in plantations.
- **Prevention over Cure:** The entire philosophy is preventative. By following the laws of health (pure air, simple diet, exercise, self-control), disease can be avoided entirely, making doctors and medicines unnecessary.

### 5. Notable Quotes

1.  **On the root of disease:** "There can be absolutely no doubt that impure air is the root-cause of disease in ninety-nine cases out of every hundred."

2.  **On the purpose of eating:** "Man is not born to eat, nor should he live to eat. His true function is to know and serve his Maker; but, since the body is essential to this service, we have perforce to eat."

3.  **On the ultimate key to health:** "Many are the keys to health, and they are all quite essential; but the one thing needful, above all others, is *Brahmacharya*... There can be no doubt that men and women can never be virile or strong unless they observe true *Brahmacharya*."
