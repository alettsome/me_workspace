# Problem Definition - Global Inbox

## The Problem

**Context loss is killing productivity and healing.**

People capture information in dozens of places:
- Notes scattered across apps (Notion, Evernote, OneNote, Apple Notes)
- Files on desktop, downloads, random folders
- Screenshots and photos in camera roll
- Thoughts in head (never captured)
- Emotions and issues (never processed)
- Email inboxes (never reach zero)
- Browser bookmarks (never revisited)
- ChatGPT conversations (may lose access)

### What Goes Wrong

**1. Forced Categorization Creates Friction**
- "Where should I put this?"
- "Is this a note or a todo?"
- "Which project does this belong to?"
- Result: Items never get captured

**2. Context Gets Lost**
- Can't remember why you saved something
- Can't find related conversations
- No trail from idea → action → outcome
- Result: Work gets abandoned

**3. Emotional Processing Gets Ignored**
- No place to process unexpected feelings
- Therapeutic journaling requires setup
- Can't share progress with practitioners
- Result: Issues never resolve

**4. Data Ownership Problems**
- ChatGPT rate-limiting blocks access to your own conversations
- Cloud services can lock you out
- Subscriptions required to access your own data
- Result: Critical information held hostage

**5. Healing ≠ Products (Missed Opportunity)**
- Personal healing journeys have value
- But logs stay private, never refined
- Could be books, courses, professional insights
- Result: One journey, zero outputs

## The Impact

**Personal Cost:**
- Stress from scattered information
- Unresolved emotional issues
- Lost ideas and insights
- Abandoned projects

**Professional Cost:**
- Can't serve clients without context
- Practitioners lack organized patient history
- No systematic knowledge capture
- Missed revenue from content creation

**Financial Cost:**
- Multiple app subscriptions
- Therapist time wasted on intake forms
- Books never written
- Courses never created

## Current "Solutions" Don't Work

**Notion/Evernote/Obsidian:**
- Require upfront categorization
- Cloud-based (data ownership issues)
- No emotional processing support
- No practitioner-sharing features

**GTD Apps (Todoist, Things):**
- Taskmaster mentality (whip analogy)
- Force everything into "tasks"
- No support for duration-agnostic items
- No journal integration

**Therapy Apps (Jour, Day One):**
- Only for journaling (no file processing)
- No connection to projects
- Can't share curated logs with practitioners
- No output generation

## What's Actually Needed

**Universal Capture:**
- Drop anything, anywhere
- No forced categorization
- System handles triage

**Context Preservation:**
- Everything connected
- Conversations stay with items
- Full history preserved

**Dual-Purpose Philosophy:**
- Capture for healing
- Refine for products
- Same journey, two outputs

**Local-First Ownership:**
- Your data, your computer
- No subscriptions required
- No rate-limiting
- Works offline

**Inbox-Zero for Everything:**
- Not just email
- Every capture point empties
- 24/7 effective resolution
- System always working

## Success Criteria

✅ **Capture friction eliminated** - Drop anything in <5 seconds  
✅ **Context never lost** - Find everything with full history  
✅ **Emotional processing smooth** - Journal entry auto-generated, start typing immediately  
✅ **Dual-purpose outputs achieved** - One journey → personal healing + marketable product  
✅ **Data ownership guaranteed** - Local-first, works offline, no subscriptions  
✅ **Revenue enabled** - Practitioner subscriptions, content products
